Impervious surface tier breakdown by school level

Each bar shows the number of schools of that type falling into each impervious surface tier. Stacked bars allow comparison of how the tier distribution differs across elementary, middle, high school, and K-8 campuses. Data source: Seattle City GIS Tree Canopy Assessment 2016–2021.

Below 55% — relatively green
55–65% — moderate concern
65–75% — high impervious
Above 75% — urgent need

55% threshold reflects the level at which urban heat island effects become a significant concern for students. 75% threshold represents the most urgent priority for green schoolyard investment.
